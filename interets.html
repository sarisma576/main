BRASILIAN JIU-JITSU

The Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (JJB) is a wrestling sport and martial art based on control techniques and ground submissions. We explain its principles and specificities. The objective is to neutralize his opponent by using techniques on the ground to obtain dominant positions or submissions (joint key, strangulation, muscle compression) forcing his opponent to abandon the fight (without having to address the slightest blow). Originally developed as a self-defense system, the JJB was revealed worldwide with the first free fights organized in the United States, by imposing itself as an effective way to defeat a stronger opponent by bringing the fight to the ground. Subsequently, JJB developed as a full-fledged sport with competitions and federations around the world.
